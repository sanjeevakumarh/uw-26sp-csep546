- Known Information:
	- $\mathbf{X} = [X_1, \ldots, X_n]^\top$ is a random vector
	- $\mathbb{E}[X_i] = \mu_i$ (mean of the $i$-th dimension/feature)
	- $\text{Cov}(X_i, X_j) = \Sigma_{i,j}$ (covariance matrix entry)
	- Based on this we can deduce
		- $\text{Var}(X_i) = \Sigma_{i,i}$ (diagonal entries = variances i.e i == j )
- **Definitions:**
	- Variance is the expected value of the squared deviation from the mean $\boldsymbol{\mu}$  of a random variable.
	- $$
	  \mathrm{Var}(X) = \mathbb{E}[(X - \mu)^2]                            \\
	  $$
	- When c is a scalar/constant  E[c] = c and E[cX] = cE[X]
	- And 
	  $$
	   \mu = \mathbb{E}[X]$$
- Step 1 : relation between $\mu$ and variance.
	- $$
	  \mathrm{Var}(X) = \mathbb{E}[(X - \mu)^2]                            \\
	  \mathrm{Var}(X) = \mathbb{E}[(X^2 - 2 X \mu+ \mu^2]   \\
	  \mathrm{Var}(X) = \mathbb{E}[X^2] - \mathbb{E}[2 X \mu] + \mathbb{E}[\mu^2]   \\
	  
	  $$
	- Step 2 : 2 & \mu are constants and E[c] = c; replace constants and simplify
		- $$
		  \mathrm{Var}(X) = \mathbb{E}[X^2] - 2\mu\mathbb{E}[X] + \mathbb{E}[\mu^2]   \\
		  \mathrm{Var}(X) = \mathbb{E}[X^2] - 2\mu^2 + \mu^2   \\
		  \mathrm{Var}(X) = \mathbb{E}[X^2] - \mu^2   \\
		   \Sigma =  \mathbb{E}[X^2] - \mu^2    \\
		   \mathbb{E}[X^2] =  \Sigma  + \mu^2    \\
		  $$
- Expression for E [ X_i^2 ] = \Sigma_{i,i} + \mu^2

  $$
  \mathbb{E}[X^\top X ] =  +  \mathbb{E}[X_1^2] +  \mathbb{E}[X_2^2] +  \mathbb{E}[X_3^2] + ......  +  \mathbb{E}[X_n^2]     \\

  $$
  \mathbb{E}[X^\top X ] = (\Sigma_{1,1} + \mu_1^2) +(\Sigma_{2,2} + \mu_2^2) +(\Sigma_{3,3} + \mu_3^2) + ........ +(\Sigma_{n,n} + \mu_n^2) \\
  \mathbb{E}[X^\top X ] = (\Sigma_{1,1} + \Sigma_{2,2} + \Sigma_{3,3} + ........ +\Sigma_{n,n} ) + ( \mu_1^2 + \mu_2^2 + \mu_3^2 + ........ + \mu_n^2)  \\
  
  $$
- $$  (\Sigma_{1,1} + \Sigma_{2,2} + \Sigma_{3,3} + ........ +\Sigma_{n,n} ) $$ is the trace of the covariance matrix.
- $$  ( \mu_1^2 + \mu_2^2 + \mu_3^2 + ........ + \mu_n^2) $$ is the dot product $$ \mu ^\top \mu $$
